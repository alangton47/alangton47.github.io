Crater Lake, located in Central Oregon next to the Umpqua National Forest, is a volcano turned lake over thousands of years. Before Crater Lake, it was a mountain named Mount Mazama. Thousands of years ago, Mount Mazama exploded, causing the top of it to blow up, leaving a massive caldera in its place that would eventually be filled up with rainwater for the next thousands of years. It is the deepest lake in the U.S., so you can imagine how much rain water is in the lake. Looking directly into Crater Lake, and you notice an island located inside. This island is known as Wizard Island, and is a cinder cone that formed inside the volcano creating the island. There are boat tours inside the Crater that can take you to Wizard Island, or other sides of the Crater.
